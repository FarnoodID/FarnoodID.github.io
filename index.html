My Approach

Iâ€™ve always been driven by a need to understand how things work. I wasn't satisfied just using software; I needed to know how a compiler translates code, how an operating system manages memory, and how data securely travels across a network.

This curiosity led me to a foundational study in computer science at Amirkabir University of Technology (Tehran Polytechnic). My focus was on "first principles": core systems, cryptography, and network security. I graduated ranked in the top 5 of my 120-person class.

Today, I'm expanding on that foundation at Politecnico di Torino, applying that same systems-level thinking to data science and machine learning. My work explores how to build intelligent systems that aren't just powerful, but also secure, efficient, and distributed. This is what draws me to complex fields like federated learning and privacy-preserving computation.

I also believe the truest measure of understanding is the ability to teach it. My time as a teaching assistant for core subjects, ranging from Data Structures & Algorithms and Operating Systems to Databases and Artificial Intelligence, wasn't just a job; it was the ultimate test of my own knowledge.

When I'm not at the keyboard, I'm usually mountain climbing, planning my next trip, or in the middle of a chess game.
